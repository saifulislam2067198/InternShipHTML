Overflow sroll

The overflow property controls what happens when content is too large to fit inside an element's box.

visible (default) – Content is not clipped and may overflow outside the box.

hidden – Extra content is clipped and not visible.

scroll – Adds scrollbars whether needed or not.

auto – Adds scrollbars only when needed.

What is the purpose of the overflow property in CSS?

The overflow property controls how content is displayed when it exceeds the size of its container, such as showing, hiding, or adding scrollbars to the extra content.
